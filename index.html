Neem (Azadirachta indica)



General Information

Scientific Name: Azadirachta indica

Family: Meliaceae (Mahogany family)

Type: Evergreen tree

Height: Typically 15–20 meters (49–66 feet)

Uses of Neem:
  • Medicinal: Widely used in Ayurveda for skin conditions, fever, and infections.
  • Insecticidal: Neem oil is a powerful natural pesticide and insect repellent.
  • Personal Care: Used in soaps, toothpaste, creams, and shampoos for its antibacterial properties.
Medicinal & Biological Uses:
  • Contains azadirachtin, nimbin, and other bioactive compounds with antimicrobial properties.
  • Leaves and bark used for treating malaria, ulcers, and skin disorders.
  • Neem oil is used in treating lice and fungal infections.
  • Warning: Neem oil should not be consumed internally without medical supervision.




Description

Neem is a fast-growing evergreen native to the Indian subcontinent, known for its medicinal, pesticidal, and environmental benefits. Its leaves are compound, the bark is rough and grayish, and its small white flowers are fragrant.

Habitat & Growth

  • Native Regions: Indian subcontinent and tropical regions
  • Soil: Thrives in well-drained, sandy to clay soils
  • Sun: Full sun exposure is ideal
  • Watering: Drought-tolerant; needs minimal watering after establishment

Propagation

Neem is propagated by seeds, which should be sown fresh for good germination. It can also be grown from stem cuttings, but seed propagation is more common in large-scale plantations.

Fun Facts & Cultural Significance

  • Neem is referred to as the "Village Pharmacy" in India due to its many uses.
  • Regarded as sacred in many Indian cultures and planted near temples and homes.
  • Neem twigs are traditionally used as toothbrushes in rural areas.
  • UN recognizes neem as a “Tree of the 21st Century” for its ecological value.
